01

Development

Track These 3 Metrics (Ignore Everything Else)

Ship Speed
PRs Merged / Week
Per dev. Aim for 3-5.
Quality
Bugs Escaped
Bugs found in prod per release
Responsiveness
Review Turnaround
Time to first review. Target: <4h.

That's it. At your scale, these three tell you everything: who's shipping, who's shipping clean, and who's unblocking others. Pull all three automatically from GitHub — zero manual entry.

Traffic Light 3+ PRs/week, <2 bugs/month, reviews <4h    1-2 PRs/week or reviews >8h    0 PRs/week or 5+ bugs escaped

Distribute Work

What Works

  • Weekly sprint with a single prioritized backlog
  • Each dev picks from top of backlog — no assignment needed
  • If blocked >2 hours, escalate immediately
  • Async standup (Slack/written) — skip the meeting

Minimize Downtime

  • Keep backlog 2 weeks ahead — nobody runs out of work
  • One dev per feature (no splitting), pair on complex ones
  • Ship small PRs (<400 lines) — faster review, faster merge
  • "You build it, you fix it" — no handoff to QA
02

Sales

Track These 4 Metrics

Output
Quota Attainment
The only metric that pays the bills
Leading Indicator
Pipeline Coverage
3x weighted pipeline = safe
Activity
Calls + Demos / Week
Input that predicts output

Quota attainment is the scoreboard. Pipeline coverage tells you if next month is safe. Activity tells you if the rep is actually working. Everything else (win rate, cycle length, etc.) is a diagnostic you look at only when something's wrong.

Traffic Light ≥100% quota, 3x+ pipeline    70-99% quota or <2x pipeline    <70% quota. Two consecutive reds = have the conversation.

Distribute Work

What Works

  • Assign territories (geo, vertical, or named accounts) — no overlap
  • Round-robin inbound leads by availability
  • Weekly pipeline review: what's closing this week, what's stuck
  • Daily activity targets, not daily check-ins

Minimize Downtime

  • When a deal closes, next lead should already be in pipeline
  • SDR books meetings directly on AE calendar — no lag
  • Set ramp targets: month 1 = 25%, month 2 = 50%, month 3 = 75%
  • If pipeline <2x, shift to outbound immediately
03

Sales Ops

Track These 3 Metrics

Core Job
Forecast Accuracy
How close was last month's call? Target: ±10%
Enablement
Rep Selling Time
% of time reps actually sell (target: 60%+, reality: 35%)
Speed
Request Turnaround
Urgent: 4h. Standard: 24h. Low: 48h.

Sales ops exists to make sales reps faster and the forecast reliable. If the forecast is accurate and reps aren't wasting time on admin, ops is doing its job.

Traffic Light Forecast within 10%, SLAs met    Forecast off by 10-20% or SLAs slipping    Forecast off >20% or chronic SLA misses

Distribute Work

  • 60/40 rule: 60% planned work (dashboards, automation, process), 40% reserved for ad-hoc requests from sales leadership
  • Ticket queue: All requests go through one intake (Slack channel or form) — not DMs. Tag priority. First in, first out within priority.
  • 2-week sprints: Plan the 60% at sprint start. Ad-hoc fills the 40% as it comes.

Marketing

Track These 4 Metrics

Revenue Impact
Pipeline Generated
$ in pipeline from marketing efforts
Efficiency
Cost Per Lead
Total spend / leads generated
Conversion
MQL → SQL Rate
Are we generating quality or garbage?
Output
Content Velocity
Pieces shipped vs. committed. On schedule?

Pipeline generated is the only metric the CEO should care about. CPL tells you if spend is efficient. MQL→SQL tells you if quality is there. Content velocity tells you if the team is executing.

Traffic Light On pipeline target, CPL stable/declining    Pipeline <80% of target or CPL rising >20%    Pipeline <50% of target or MQL→SQL <5%

Distribute Work

What Works

  • Monthly campaign calendar — everyone sees the plan
  • 2-week sprints for execution (same as dev)
  • Each campaign has one owner, clear deliverables, clear deadline
  • Retro after every major campaign (what worked, what didn't, 15 min max)

Minimize Downtime

  • Keep a "ready to launch" queue of 2-3 campaigns
  • Approval SLAs: 24h for content, 48h for legal/brand review
  • Templates for everything: briefs, emails, landing pages
  • No one waits for approvals — escalate after SLA expires
05

Finance

Track These 3 Metrics

Speed
Close Time
Days to close the books. Target: 5 days.
Accuracy
Forecast Variance
Actual vs. budget. Target: <5%.
Cash Health
AR Aging
% of receivables >60 days. Target: <10%.

Finance is about speed + accuracy. Close fast, close clean, collect cash. If those three are green, the finance team is doing its job.

Traffic Light Close ≤5 days, variance <5%, AR >60d under 10%    Close 6-8 days, variance 5-10%    Close >8 days, variance >10%, or AR >60d above 20%

Distribute Work

  • Close calendar is the Bible: Every task, every owner, every deadline. Published on day 1 of the month. No ambiguity.
  • Pre-close (days -5 to 0): Reconcile high-volume accounts before the period ends. Don't wait for month-end.
  • FP&A cycles: Monthly variance analysis (mandatory), quarterly reforecast, annual plan. Keep it simple — one model, one source of truth.
  • Ad-hoc requests: Business units get self-service dashboards for common questions. Only custom analysis goes through the finance queue.
06

Legal

Track These 3 Metrics

Speed
Contract Turnaround
NDA: 1-2 days. Standard: 3-5 days. Complex: 2 weeks.
Volume
Open Matters
Per attorney. Are we overloaded or underutilized?
Satisfaction
Internal NPS
Quarterly 3-question survey from business teams

Legal is a service team. Track how fast they serve (turnaround), how much they can handle (volume), and whether the business is happy with the service (NPS). That's the whole picture.

Traffic Light All SLAs met, NPS >8/10    1-2 SLA misses/month or NPS 6-8    Chronic SLA misses or NPS <6

Distribute Work

What Works

  • Single intake form — not DMs, not emails, one channel
  • Auto-route: NDAs go to junior, litigation to senior
  • Self-service for standard contracts (templates + playbook)
  • Weekly triage meeting (15 min): what's open, what's stuck, what's urgent

Minimize Downtime

  • Template library kills 60% of routine requests
  • Escalation rule: if no response in SLA, it auto-escalates
  • Batch similar matters (all NDAs on Tuesday, all vendor reviews Thursday)
  • Clear priority tiers: urgent (litigation), important (deals), routine (policies)

How Task Distribution Works Across All Departments Every department follows the same pattern: (1) One prioritized queue of work. (2) Clear ownership — every task has one name on it. (3) Defined SLAs — everyone knows "fast enough." (4) Escalation rules — blocked work surfaces automatically, not when someone remembers to mention it. The queue format varies (sprint backlog, deal pipeline, close calendar, intake form) but the principle is identical.
Carlos's Red Rule Two consecutive reds in any department = mandatory conversation. Three reds = action plan or separation. This applies to every department equally — whether it's a sales rep missing quota, a developer not shipping, or a finance analyst missing close deadlines. One standard, one system.
